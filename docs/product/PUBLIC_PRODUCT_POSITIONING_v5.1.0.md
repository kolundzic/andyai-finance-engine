# AndyAI Finance Engine v5.1.0 — Public Product Positioning Layer

## Product Definition

AndyAI Finance Engine is a governed financial modeling workspace for founders, consultants, agencies, and business teams.

It helps transform business inputs, assumptions, pricing ideas, SaaS scenarios, investor logic, and operational plans into structured financial model drafts.

## Core Promise

Build financial models faster, but keep responsibility visible.

AndyAI Finance Engine is not designed to replace human financial judgment.

It is designed to help humans structure, verify, explain, and approve financial thinking.

## Target Users

| User Type | Use Case |
|---|---|
| Founders | SaaS pricing, cost planning, runway thinking, investor preparation |
| Consultants | Client financial scenarios, offer modeling, proposal support |
| Agencies | Productized service pricing, monthly retainers, revenue planning |
| Small teams | Shared assumptions, model review, approval flow |
| Enterprise pilots | Governance, audit, controlled financial modeling workflows |

## What Makes It Different

AndyAI Finance Engine is trust-first.

The product is not only about generating numbers.

It focuses on:

- Assumption visibility
- Human approval
- Audit trail
- Review status
- Export safety
- Monetization boundaries
- Provider-neutral billing design

## Monetization Status

v5.1.0 is not a live billing release.

Live payments remain disabled.

No checkout route, webhook, payment provider SDK, subscription enforcement, payment secret, or paid export runtime is active.

## Current Foundation

The product now has:

- Monetization gate foundation
- Pricing and offer matrix
- Membership access policy
- Usage quota policy
- Billing adapter contract
- Safety and export rules
- Post-release seal
- Public product positioning layer

## Strategic Direction

The next major direction is Supabase monetization setup.

That future layer should introduce database-backed membership, quota, audit, and approval foundations without breaking the trust-first architecture.

## Canonical Line

Financial modeling can be automated.

Financial responsibility cannot be outsourced.
